Wow, it has already been a week. That's kind of nuts. Don't know how I feel about that. [Time](https://youtu.be/WuXwSyahgW4?si=IvT6sA0ikutxUPDs) keeps on [slipping](https://youtu.be/pxd_eDTbBMQ?si=AJPhh4nnpKHWmWmu).

For context, I am currently in the small borough of [Patton, Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patton,_Pennsylvania). I came here to attend the [Art14 Residency](https://www.lastudio.org/art14-residency/residency). My goal over these three weeks is to create an album of music and to make some progress on locative.garden, my long unfinished collection of fiction. I am here from 2026-04-11 to 2026-05-02.

I am also trying to recover a bit from some general work burnout. I am able to do this residency because [Laurel](https://laurel.ai) historically has had a one month sabbatical once you reach five years of employment. Sadly after this year it's going away, so I'm taking advantage of it while I can. 

This week I did a bunch of stuff. One thing I'm fighting against is when I get stuck with writing or playing, I dive back into general code stuff. I do like this from a pure dopamine hit, but often it also makes me frustrated. My point of this time off is to focus on skills that I am not great at, or regularly don't focus on because of time. So going to things that I do during my normal day to day feels good because I am good at them, but most times it's happened I regret it.

In the category of random coding stuff:

- Put a landing page at [subalpinedrift.com](https://subalpinedrift.com/).
- Made some improvements to [robot.villas](https://robot.villas).
- Cleaned up my github, archived 240 old repos.
- Made some pull requests to [wayback](https://github.com/JamieMagee/wayback) to fix a GitHub Action I have on this blog.
- Fixed some GitHub Actions for the [norns-community repo](https://github.com/monome/norns-community/pull/378).

In music making, I made music every day! A lot of it was general experimentation and teaching myself more. One area I am particularly learning a lot about is [MIDI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIDI), something I historically have avoided... why I have no idea. Other things I have done:

- Published my [Modstock 2025 performance on Bandcamp](https://subalpinedrift.bandcamp.com/).
- Made and published a Norns script for generating MIDI: [grurder](https://github.com/icco/grurder/).
- Published the first track of my [weekly sound experiment, Drifts](https://subalpinedrift.bandcamp.com/album/drifts). This is something I've wanted to do since the new year, but this focus time got me off my ass.
- Finished first track of my album.

I didn't do too much writing. I journaled every single day, a habit I used to have that I want to get back. I also wrote 320 words in locative.garden... and deleted 664... Next week more writing!

I also have been listening to [The Creative Act by Rick Rubin](https://sites.prh.com/thecreativeact). I've been listening to this for the last few months, and I regularly restart chapters or sections. It often takes me a little bit to get into, as  Rubin's spirituality has always felt performative to me. He's been buddhist since he was 14 though so who am I to judge? Anyways, the book is great. Has inspired me a bunch in how I think about creating, and I really connect his point that everyone is creative and can be an artist.
